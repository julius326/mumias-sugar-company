ABOUT US

Mumias Sugar Company Limited is a leading sugar manufacturing company in Kenya, the largest economy in the East African Community. It is the country’s largest sugar producer, accounting for about 250,000 metric tonnes (42%) of Kenya’s annual output of 600,000 metric tonnes.

Location

The company’s headquarters are located in Mumias town, Kakamega County, Western Kenya—close to its sugar plantations and factory facilities. Mumias is approximately 395 km (245 miles) by road from Nairobi. An operations center is also maintained in Nairobi, Kenya’s capital city.

Overview

Mumias Sugar Company is primarily engaged in the manufacture and sale of sugar. The company grows some sugarcane itself (about 7% of annual output), but its main supply comes from over 50,000 registered out-growers cultivating more than 400 square kilometers (99,000 acres). In partnership with the UN Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), the company has also piloted high-yield palm oil varieties in areas previously unsuitable for cultivation.

Beyond sugar, the company co-generates 34 Megawatts of electricity, using part of it internally while selling surplus to the national grid. It also manufactures 24 million liters of ethanol annually and 20 million liters of distilled water.

History

Mumias Sugar Company was founded in 1971 with the following shareholding profile: Government of Kenya (71%), Commonwealth Development Corporation UK (17%), Kenya Commercial Finance Company (5%), Booker McConnel (4%), and East African Development Bank (3%). The company’s peak sugar output was in 2005, reaching 269,184 metric tonnes.

Governance

The Board of Directors is chaired by Dan Ameyo (non-executive director), while Errol Johnson serves as Chief Executive Officer.

Objectives

The company was established with the following key objectives:

  • Provide a reliable source of income for farmers
  • Create employment opportunities in the region
  • Reduce rural-urban migration
  • Promote self-sufficiency in sugar production and reduce imports
  • Operate on a commercial basis and generate profits

Recent Developments

Since 2012, Mumias Sugar Company has faced declining output and profits, initially attributed to inefficiencies. A KPMG forensic audit uncovered financial irregularities and losses exceeding KSh 1 billion. This led to the dismissal of 52 senior managers, including the CEO and CFO. The company sought a government bailout of KSh 2.3 billion, of which KSh 500 million was disbursed in February 2015.

In September 2019, Kenya Commercial Bank (KCB), owed KSh 545 million, placed the company under receivership and appointed PVR Rao as administrator. In December 2021, Sarrai Group was awarded the lease as the successful bidder, but multiple court challenges followed, citing irregularities in the bidding process. By April 2022, Justice Mabeya canceled the lease award, citing lack of creditor consultation and failure to comply with competition regulations. A new administrator, Mr. Kereto Marima, was appointed to restart the process.
